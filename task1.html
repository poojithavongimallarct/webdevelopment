Birds

  • Sparrow: A small, brown bird that is common in many parts of the world.

    Sparrows are known for their chirping songs and their ability to build nests in a variety of places. They are also very adaptable birds and can live in a wide range of habitats.

  • Pigeon: A medium-sized bird that is often seen in urban areas.

    Pigeons are known for their cooing calls and their ability to travel long distances. They are also considered to be pests in some areas due to their droppings.

  • Eagle: A large, powerful bird of prey.

    Eagles are known for their sharp talons and their ability to soar high in the sky. They are also considered to be symbols of power and strength.

  • Penguin: A flightless bird that lives in Antarctica.

    Penguins are known for their waddling gait and their ability to swim underwater. They are also considered to be symbols of cold weather and the Antarctic.

  • Ostrich: The largest bird in the world.

    Ostriches are known for their long legs and their ability to run fast. They are also considered to be symbols of Africa and the savanna.

  • Kiwi: A flightless bird that is native to New Zealand.

    Kiwis are known for their long beaks and their ability to sense insects underground. They are also considered to be symbols of New Zealand and its unique wildlife.

  • Parrot: A colorful bird that is known for its ability to mimic human speech.

    Parrots are known for their bright colors and their ability to learn to talk. They are also considered to be symbols of intelligence and companionship.
